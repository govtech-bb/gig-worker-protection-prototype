# Why the prototype works this way — a page-by-page guide for NISSS

This explains the design thinking behind each screen, so NISSS can see that every choice traces back to either the research findings, the AIDA journey we agreed, or the Ann-Marie King discovery session.

**Three principles run through every page:**
1. **Protection you buy, not tax you owe.** Every page leads with what the worker *gets*. This is the report's single biggest framing fix ("It's just a tax" was named the #1 communications failure).
2. **Voluntary, never enforcement.** No surveillance, no chasing, no "you must." We say so out loud, repeatedly.
3. **No number we can't stand behind.** Where a benefit amount isn't confirmed by NISSS, we show *"to be confirmed with NISSS"* rather than guess. The report flags this as the #1 risk: "showing a wrong number is worse than no number."

The core journey (shown in the progress bar) is five steps: **Work → Earnings → Risk → Plan → Next steps**. Around it sit a few supporting and branching pages.

---

## 1. Landing — *AIDA: Attention*
**"Protect your income. Protect your future."**

- **Why it leads with protection, not the scheme.** Workers told us they've heard of NIS but can't say what it does for *them*. So the headline is about *them*, not the institution.
- **"From NISSS" badge.** Trust is inherited, not advertised — people trust NIS because family and older workers vouched for it. Attaching the page visibly to NISSS and the gov.bb brand borrows that trust; it does not try to win a head-to-head against private insurance (which is actively distrusted — CLICO still comes up).
- **Plain "What is NISSS, simply?" box.** The information gap is specific, not general. One short, jargon-free explanation closes it.
- **"It's voluntary, no penalty for slow months."** Pre-empts the "I can't pay every month" objection right at the door.
- **Three entry buttons** (see protection / estimate / register) let people at different readiness levels self-select instead of forcing everyone down one path.
- **"Nothing here is shared with BRA or used to chase you."** The first of several deliberate BRA-separation statements — this is the "it's just a tax" reframe in action.

---

## 2. What kind of work do you do? — *AIDA: Attention → recognition*

- **Why it's first in the journey.** The report's Attention stage is "workers recognise themselves in the list" (driver, host, freelancer, vendor, trades). Seeing your own work named is what makes a stranger believe the page is for them.
- **It asks about *work*, not a *business*.** This quietly dismantles the most action-blocking misconception — "I have to register a business first" (Participant 2 hadn't contributed for years because of it). We never ask for a business name.

---

## 3. Let's talk about your earnings — *personalisation step*

- **Good month / slow month sliders.** Self-employed income is uneven; a single "monthly salary" question would feel wrong and exclude people. Two numbers capture the real pattern.
- **"This stays on your phone."** Reassurance before we ask anything financial — trust is fragile here, and we collect *no* data until the very end.
- **Dependants question.** Feeds the risk figures on the next page (a sole earner with children has a very different exposure).
- **Why it comes before the risk screen.** This was a deliberate reorder. The risk figures are only persuasive if they use *the worker's own numbers* — so earnings must be captured first.

---

## 4. What if something happened tomorrow? — *AIDA: Desire (the Risk Audit)*

- **This is the Solopreneur Risk Audit the report recommends.** It answers the Desire-stage question: *"What happens if I don't have this, to me, today?"*
- **The numbers are the worker's own.** "Two weeks sick ≈ $X you couldn't earn"; "at 65, that's $Y/year from somewhere else." Because this runs *after* the earnings step, the gap is personal, not generic — which is the whole point of the Audit.
- **Directly targets "It will never happen to me."** Younger, healthier workers repeated this; making the cost visible in BBD is the documented answer.
- **Without / With NISSS contrast** on each scenario keeps the framing on what you gain, not what you owe.

---

## 5. What should you put in? — *AIDA: Desire → Action bridge*

- **Tiers built from the worker's income.** Reflects Ann-Marie's Adaptive Contribution Model — you choose a level that fits what you earn, with the $1,200/year floor as the entry point.
- **Protection shown as a level, not a fake percentage.** We removed invented "40% / 70%" figures and show "Basic / Better / Strongest cover" instead, with the bar as a relative indicator only. This honours the "no wrong numbers" rule.
- **Each benefit carries "(amount to be confirmed with NISSS)".** Benefit *names and meaning* are safe to show; benefit *amounts* must come from the NISSS self-employed unit. The placeholder makes the page shippable today and obviously honest.
- **"When your cover starts" card** (one full year of contributions) sets a realistic expectation — flagged for NISSS confirmation.
- **"Slow month? Pay less or skip — no penalty."** The adaptive model again, addressing uneven income head-on.

---

## 6. Ready to take the next step? — *AIDA: Action*

- **Three routes, because channel preference splits sharply.** Older workers and some migrants prefer to walk in and speak to someone; younger and remote workers are digital-first. So we offer: register now, see how to pay, or *have someone call me*. No one is forced online.
- **"Joining is voluntary, you choose how much."** Reinforced at the moment of commitment.

---

## 7. Do you already have an NISSS number? — *branching*

- **Routes existing vs new contributors.** Many self-employed people contributed through a past employer and don't realise the number is theirs for life. Getting this right avoids duplicate registrations and confusion.

---

## 8. How to register — *Action support; the "It's not too late" page*

- **Three channels: online (nis.gov.bb), in person, by phone.** Matches the channel findings; the walk-in route is first-class, not an afterthought.
- **We link out to nis.gov.bb — we don't rebuild registration.** Per the report's "what we are not building": duplicating the real form would create a second source of truth and a worse experience.
- **Surcharge tip + grant pathway.** This is the "It's not too late" content page: back-pay up to 3 years, the 5% surcharge per missed year shown plainly, and the grant pathway for people near retirement (Participant 4, 67, believed she was too late — she isn't).

---

## 9. How to pay — *adaptive payment*

- **SurePay / EZpay+ and the catch-up rules.** Shows the adaptive model in practice: weekly, monthly, lump sums, or catch up — NISSS already permits this, but almost no worker knows.
- **Surcharge note** repeats the "sooner is cheaper" message consistently.

---

## 10. Tell us where to reach you — *the only data-collection page*

- **This is the first and only place we collect anything.** The agreed principle is "no data until the Action stage; everything before is information." That keeps the earlier pages free of privacy/legal review and lets them ship fast.
- **Minimal fields + strong reassurance** ("not shared with BRA, employers, or anyone else"). This is the interest-signal capture that hands over to the NISSS self-employed unit.

---

## 11. Confirmation

- **Reference number + what happens next.** Closes the loop and sets the expectation that a real person follows up — consistent with the voluntary, supportive tone.

---

## Supporting pages

**Six ways NISSS protects you** *(benefits quick-read)* — the report's Interest-stage "What you get for $1,200 a year" page, in plain language. A fast alternative for people who want the benefits before the journey.

**Track my NISSS contributions** *(tracker)* — kept deliberately as a **preview of a future feature**. Workers don't currently know how much they've put in or what they qualify for; this shows the idea. A banner states plainly that the data is illustrative and the feature is future, so no one mistakes it for a live service.

---

## What we deliberately did *not* do

- **No rebuilt registration form** — we deep-link to the real one at nis.gov.bb.
- **No EZpay+ replacement** — out of scope; we link.
- **No employer/platform reporting tool** — the Moore Resolution is still a Resolution, not law; building it now would be premature and would break the voluntary framing.
- **No invented benefit figures** — see the companion `NISSS-questions.md` for the exact amounts we need NISSS to confirm before those placeholders go live.

---

*Prototype is not connected to real NISSS systems and stores no personal data. Companion document: `NISSS-questions.md` (the figures and rules awaiting NISSS sign-off).*
